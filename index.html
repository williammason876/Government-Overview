    The founding fathers decided that when writing the Constitution that they should balance the power of the overnment by creating three different branches that could check the power of the other two. The Legislative Branch is made up of two chambers the House of Representatives and the Senate; The Executive branch is made up of the President, his cabinet, and several other independent agencies such as the FBI and CIA; while the Judicial Branch is made up of the federal judges appointed a variety of levels ranging from the Supreme Court all the way down to Federal District Courts.
